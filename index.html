Biography

Anaelia Ovalle (they/she) is a computer science doctoral student at UCLA. They are currently obsessing over the intersections of representation learning and algorithmic fairness. Elia is conscious of context-sensitive impacts and implications upon deployment, with particular interests in improving health equity and empowering historically marginalized populations. Able to see the big picture, they have hands-on experience as a data scientist leading teams to build end-to-end machine learning solutions for internal and external stakeholders.

They lead the UCLA Graduate Women in Computer Science organization, passionate about supporting womxn engineers in their industrial and academic endeavors.

Selected Publications

Harms of gender exclusivity and challenges in non-binary representation in language technologies

Gender is widely discussed in the context of language tasks and when examining the stereotypes propagated by language models. However, current discussions primarily treat gender as binary, which can perpetuate harms such as the cyclical erasure of non-binary gender identities. (EMNLP 2021)
PDF Code Project

Leveraging Social Media Activity and Machine Learning for HIV and Substance Abuse Risk Assessment

A data collection and artificial intelligence system for automated health risk behavior assessment using social media data. (JMIR 2021)

A Smartwatch-Based Medication Adherence Monitoring System Using Machine Learning and Distributed Computing.

A machine learning driven smartwatch application that collects data from embedded inertial sensors, such its accelerometer and gyroscope, to monitor and track a series of actions happening during an individual’s medication intake. (EMBS 2018)

Applying Deep Learning to Public Health: Using Unbalanced Demographic Data to Predict Thyroid Disorder

Applying deep learning techniques to select candidates for early intervention for improved health outcomes, utilizing a large dataset with only minimal demographic variables, similar to datasets that are held by the marketing arms of healthcare providers. (IEMCON 2018)
